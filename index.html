Case Study

Nonprofit Website Redesign:
Portland Metro Beekeeping Association

The Portland Metro Beekeepers Association (PMBA) relies on donations, membership, and volunteers to provide resources to the community on bees and beekeeping, however, it competes with multiple local beekeeping associations and may not be attracting new members or website interest.

The Solution

Increase interest in the PMBA website by designing a space that has a clear mission, lots of resources, and feels welcoming to users and potential future members.

My Role

User Research, Information Architecture, Interface Design, User Testing

Background

PMBA is a volunteer-run Portland Beekeeping Association that has been meeting since 1990, and is committed to sharing their knowledge and expertise around all things bee with others, by offering resources and information to help people of all skill levels learn more about bees and beekeeping.

Research

To first discern what users may want from their local beekeeping association our group dove into research and posted a segmented survey on Reddit and conducted five 1-1 user interviews. We were able to utilize data from both the survey and interviews to form a clearer picture of our user. We then conducted a competitor analysis of direct and indirect competitors to help gain insights on where PMBA sits within the beekeeping marketplace.

We utilized data from our interviews and survey responses to create our user persona: a user who is looking to become an amateur beekeeper, wants to learn by getting hands-on, and wants to develop community relationships.

Problem Identification

Amateur beekeepers need access to local beekeeping workshops and ways to connect with local experienced mentors in order to learn beekeeping fundamentals. Beekeeping associations want to provide a resourceful and welcoming community to other local beekeepers and bee enthusiasts, and therefore need to increase membership to be able to provide support to their members.

With the needs of both the user and the stakeholder in mind we focused on 4 major goals for the redesign: To increase membership, improve community engagement, increase revenue, and improve access to resources

We began brainstorming as a group, asking how might we help increase membership so that users could successfully:

  • Get educational resources
  • Connect with members of their community
  • Find supplies and products

Iterating Towards a solution

After conducting a heuristic analysis, and restructuring the information architecture, we began prototyping initial wireframes for responsive web and mobile prototypes.

After mocking up an initial lo-fi prototype and getting some quick feedback, we moved forward with creating a hi-fi version. Our initial heuristic evaluation found issues with contrast and consistency, so we decided to update the color palette, imagery and logo. The decision to change the color palette was based on not only the contrast testing but the inconsistency of its use in the current design. We ultimately decided to iterate the warm tones that are reminiscent of honey and bees and add a purple that is reminiscent of flowers, and is reportedly a bee's favorite color.

Iteration: Testing

We created 4 objectives to test the usability of our prototype via moderated online observations. Users were 100% successful on 3 of the 4 tasks. One unsuccessful attempt lead us to iterate our honeycomb buttons by adding explanatory text, helping to direct our users too click the icons for more info. We also conducted A/B testing to gather additional user preference data related to button size.

Solution

Whenever possible, we added interactions to make browsing and interacting with the site as user friendly and interesting as possible. We also wanted to incorporate whimsy and continue the honeycomb theme throughout.

The next steps in this project would be:

  • Reconnecting with stakeholders and reviewing our design
  • Obtain existing site analytics and iterate based on current use data
  • Testing our prototype on active PMBA members
  • Continuing prototype iteration based on feedback
  • Create formal social media plan to expand internet presence
  • Connecting with developers to implement design
